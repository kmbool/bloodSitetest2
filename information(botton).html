Who can donate blood?

All people can donate blood if they are in good health. There are some basic requirements that must be met to donate blood. Below are some basic guidelines for eligibility to donate blood:

Age The age of the donor ranges between 18 and 65 years. National legislation in some countries permits people between the ages of 16 and 17 to donate blood provided they meet the necessary physical and blood standards and that appropriate consent can be obtained. Regular blood donors over the age of 65 may be accepted in some countries at the discretion of the physician in charge. The maximum age for donating blood in some countries is 60 years.

Additional information on eligibility to donate blood

  1. National guidelines on eligibility must be followed when people donate blood to blood transfusion services in specific countries. To find out if there are any health problems, medications, professions or travel history that may affect an individual's eligibility to donate blood, please turn to the national or local departments of blood transfusion services for detailed information in this regard.
